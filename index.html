What Are Smart Cities?

The different Smart Indexes for city were determined by the Leap Data team in an attempt to
quantify how efficient, modern and well run each city is. This was determined by analysing
multiple public datasets that included information about each city. The "smarter" a city is
in a specific category, means it will have a higher index in that category.

Note: the Smart City Index is not necessarily based on the total sum of each individual index
as some indexes were deemed more import than others. What classifies as a city is based
on each government's classification. This list does not include all cities in the world
but only the top 96 Smartest Cities.

Smart Cities Around The World

As you can see from the dot map, the majority of Smart Cities are located in
Europe, North America and Asia-Pacific. Europe has by far the highest density
of Smart Cities most likely because many of these cities are wealthy, have relatively
small populations and high education rates leading to more development and
modernising of their cities.

Finland has 9 Smart Cities despite having a relatively small population and GDP
whilst Japan, only has 2 smart Cities with its enormous population and GDP.
Of course it is possible that this is due to the way a Smart City is calculated or
determined, however it is important to note the differences between
countries such as Finland and Japan to see what makes a country have more or
less Smart Cities.

Affects of Population on Smart Indexes

Shown in the scatter plot matrix, is how the Smart Indexes change with the cities population.
The Smart People Index, appears to be relatively randomly distributed, with population showing
no apparent effect. Smart Government, Smart Environment and to some extent, Smart Economy
appear to decrease as population increases. This is likely due to large cities having more government
officials and layers of government which makes it more difficult to effectively lead and use their money
well. Larger cities also tend to be more poluted and harder to install renewables such as wind turbines
and hydroelectric power plants as all the space is taken up by housing.

Smart Mobility tends to increase as population increases, possibly due to more people resulting
in a greater need for effective and smart transportation services to facilitate the large number of
people. The Smart City Index appears to be uneffected by population with cities of all size
having an average score around 6000.

How Does Melbourne Compare To Other Smart Cities?

Melbourne often ranks highly on the most liveable and best cities in the world and still ranks quite highly
in the Smart Cities Index. With a Smart City Index of 6061, Melbourne is ranked 54th on this list, suggesting that
Melbourne is the 54th Smartest City in the world. Melbourne ranks closely with other Oceanic cities, such as
